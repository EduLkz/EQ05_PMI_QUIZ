Texto I

I, Too

I, too, sing America.
I am the darker brother.
They send me to eat in the kitchen
When company comes,
But I laugh,
And eat well,
And grow strong.
Tomorrow,
I’ll be at the table
When company comes.
Nobody’ll dare
Say to me,
“Eat in the kitchen,”
Then.
Besides,
They’ll see how beautiful I am
And be ashamed—
I, too, am America.
(Langston Hugues, 1925)


RAMPERSED, A. (ed). The Collected Works of Langston Hughes.v. 1. Poems 1921-1940. Columbia & London: University of Missouri, 2001.
